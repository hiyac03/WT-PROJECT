MODEL DETAILS & REFERENCES



In the ever-evolving landscape of healthcare, the fusion of cutting-edge technology and medical expertise is driving unprecedented advancements. This project harnesses the power of Machine Learning (ML) and Deep Learning (DL) to redefine disease detection and diagnosis. At its core, this project aims to construct an integrated framework capable of detecting and diagnosing a wide spectrum of medical conditions, thus augmenting the abilities of medical professionals and improving patient outcomes.


Brain tumor detection and segmentation have been fully operationalized, enhancing the accuracy and efficiency of brain-related disease diagnosis. Additionally, the system is adept at identifying retinal diseases, thus reducing the risks associated with vision impairment and blindness. These initial successes have opened the door to a future where a vast array of diseases can be swiftly and accurately identified, offering timely intervention and personalized treatment plans.

ResNet

Residual Networks work on the principal of skip connections as input values are fast-forwarded to subsequent layers. This increases the models generalizing capabilities which other model structures struggle with as they get deeper.

UNet

U-Nets are specialized convolutional neural networks for precise image segmentation tasks. They comprise a contracting path for context and an expanding path for localization, making them highly effective, especially in medical image analysis.

Read more

DenseNet

DenseNets are convolutional neural networks with densely connected layers, allowing for efficient parameter utilization and improved learning. This architecture excels in tasks like image classification and object detection.
